## Post 1: Retainers vs Performance-Based Pricing

I see agency owners chasing monthly retainers because they're looking for predictable income. But retainers can actually be an income limiter in your business if you intend on getting profoundly rich.

Let's do some basic math.

You have 10 clients paying $5,000/month each. That's $50,000 in monthly recurring revenue. Sounds great.

But the problem is you're now capped.

You can't charge that client $15,000 next month just because you delivered exceptional results.

You also can't bill them $25,000 because their campaign generated $500,000 in revenue.

So now you're stuck at $5,000 unless you come up with an upsell that delivers even MORE work for them.

Meanwhile, the client is making hundreds of thousands of dollars from your work, and you're collecting the same flat fee regardless of performance.

This creates a shitty incentive structure. The better you perform for clients, the worse your economics become.

Here's a hypothetical example: You build a funnel for a client that generates $100,000 in the first month. Your retainer? Still $5,000.

Month two, you optimize it and it generates $200,000. Your retainer? Still $5,000.

Month three, you scale the ads and it generates $400,000. Your retainer? Still $5,000.

You just helped this client make $700,000 in three months, and you collected $15,000 total.

That's a 2.1% take of the value you created.

Now let's look at the same scenario with a different pricing model.

Instead of a $5,000 retainer, you charge a $3,000 base fee plus 10% of attributable gross revenue.

Month one: $3,000 + (10% × $100,000) = $13,000

Month two: $3,000 + (10% × $200,000) = $23,000

Month three: $3,000 + (10% × $400,000) = $43,000

Total: $79,000 instead of $15,000.

That's nearly 3x more money for the exact same work.

Of course, people are scared of performance-based pricing because they don't trust their ability to deliver results. They'd rather have the "safety" of a guaranteed $5,000 than risk making $2,000 or $20,000 based on performance.

But this is backwards thinking.

If you can't consistently deliver results that justify performance-based pricing, you shouldn't be running an agency in the first place.

But when you have systematized, AI-powered processes that consistently deliver results (without relying on your own personal abilities), performance-based pricing becomes much easier.

Maybe you've never built funnels or run ads before. But when you use AI, the learning curve goes down from 300 hours to 20 hours. You become immediately capable of delivering copy, funnels, and ads that actually convert in 1/15th the learning time.

---

## Key Insight
Revenue models should align with the value delivered; performance-based pricing allows businesses to capture more upside from successful outcomes.

## Supporting Observation
Flat retainers cap earning potential and create misaligned incentives between service providers and clients.
