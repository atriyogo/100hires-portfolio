## Post 1: AI, Coordination, and Changing Org Structures

Maybe you *shouldn't* take that mid-manager promotion...

Anyone else read the Jack Dorsey essay, "From Hierarchy to Intelligence"?

It provides a lovely history lesson on org design, but shows how Block is using AI to rewrite the playbook.

Here's the TL;DR - middle management has historically been required to scale the coordination of people. AI makes it possible to offload this coordination tax.

Flatter orgs: Specialists, folks responsible for outcomes, player coaches

(Sellers... you're safe)

The model is simple:
1) The world model: This is the coordinating layer  
2) The capabilities: These are the micro tasks that combine to tackle jobs to be done  
3) The intelligence layer: The self improving brain processing the data running through capabilities  
4) Surfaces / Interfaces: Where the intelligence connects with users  

---

## Key Insight
AI is reducing the need for coordination-heavy roles by automating information flow and decision-making, enabling more efficient and outcome-focused systems.

## Supporting Observation
Organizations can operate with fewer layers when intelligence and coordination are handled programmatically instead of manually.

---

## Relevance to Cold Outreach Pipeline

- Cold outreach systems are moving toward:
  - automation (AI-driven workflows)
  - data-driven decision making
  - reduced manual coordination
- Teams can scale outbound with:
  - fewer people
  - better tooling
  - smarter systems
- High-performing outreach relies on **system design**, not just human effort

---

## Pattern Noticed Across Content

1. Systems and automation replace manual coordination  
2. Efficiency comes from intelligent workflows  
3. AI enables scale without proportional team growth  
4. Outbound performance increasingly depends on tooling and infrastructure
