## Post 1: Identity vs Observation

A friend told me to read the Tao Te Ching.

I got halfway through.

Then I said:

“I don’t think I’m smart enough to understand this.”

That’s the moment the clay gets pressed.

Once? 
No big deal.

But press it enough…

and the clay hardens.

Now it’s not a passing thought.

It’s “who I am.”

Here’s the shift I’m learning:

Instead of:

“I’m not smart enough”

Try:

“This passage feels difficult to understand.”

Same moment.
Different meaning.

One turns it into identity.
The other keeps it as an observation.

Why does that matter?

Because identity feels permanent.
Observation feels workable.

“I’m not smart enough”
→ fixed
→ heavy
→ no way out

“This is hard to understand”
→ temporary
→ specific
→ invites curiosity

One shuts the door.

The other cracks it open.

---

## Key Insight
How you frame a situation internally affects your ability to respond effectively. Separating identity from observation keeps you flexible and open to improvement.

## Supporting Observation
Rigid self-labeling (“I’m not good at this”) reduces action, while neutral observation (“this is difficult”) encourages problem-solving and curiosity.

## Post 2: Handling Resistance in Cold Calls

Chances are, you’ve heard this on a cold call:

“What do you want?!”

Said with a little edge.

Don’t get flustered.  
Don’t slip into a pitch.  
Don’t Voss them with, “Sounds like you get a lot of these calls.”

Lean back.  
Pause.  
Then answer the question calmly.

“I’m calling because I had a question about how you’re scheduling jobs that come in after hours.”

That’s it.

Why it works:

They’re asking for clarity.

“Why are you calling?”

The moment people feel like you’re hiding the reason for the call, resistance goes up.

The moment you answer directly, tension drops.

Now they can decide whether it’s worth continuing.

Sometimes the fastest way to lower resistance  
is simply answering the question.

---

## Key Insight
Direct and transparent communication reduces resistance in cold outreach.

## Supporting Observation
Prospects react negatively when they sense hidden intent. Clarity lowers tension and increases the chance of continuing the conversation.
