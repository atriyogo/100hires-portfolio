## Post 1: Sales Process vs Buying Process

There's a sales process. 

And there's a buying process.

They're not the same thing.

Here's where most reps mess this up:

We love discovery calls. Buyers hate showing up just to answer questions for 30 minutes without seeing anything.

We want to wait on proof of concept. Buyers might want to prove it works before they go any further.

We have our timeline. They have theirs.

The sales process is what we want to happen.

The buying process is what actually needs to happen for them to buy.

And if those two don't match?

Friction. Frustration. Stalled deals.

The best reps don't force their process onto the buyer.

They learn the buying process — and become a guide through it.

→ Suggest what the next step should be (don't just ask)  
→ Tell them who typically gets involved and why  
→ Create cheat sheets for the people they'll need to sell internally  

You sell this product every day.

They buy it once.

They might not know the best way to buy it.

Your job is to help them.

---

## Key Insight
Successful sales and outreach depend on aligning with the buyer’s process, not forcing an internal sales process.

## Supporting Observation
Mismatch between sales process and buying process creates friction, slows deals, and reduces conversion rates.

---

## Relevance to Cold Outreach Pipeline

- Cold outreach should focus on **helping the buyer move forward**, not pushing a predefined funnel
- Messaging should reflect:
  - buyer intent
  - buyer timing
  - buyer context
- Positioning outreach as **guidance** increases trust and engagement
