## Post 1: AI Adoption > AI Tools

The hardest part of AI in agencies isn't the technology. It's the adoption.

Every week there's a new tool, a new model, a better way to do something.

And every week, agency leaders face the same tension:

Do we update the workflow to capture the efficiency?
Or do we protect the process that's actually delivering for clients right now?

Based on what I see across our own agencies, the tools aren't the bottleneck.

Your team's willingness to change how they work is.

I have a ton of conversations with other agency owners. I'm in several communities.

And through what I've heard and witnessed, many agencies buy every AI subscription on the market and still operate closely like they did in 2024. Meanwhile, I've seen lean teams with basic tools completely transform their output because they actually adopted new ways of working.

The difference isn't budget. It's culture.

From my seat and what I've gathered, here's what I believe actually moves the needle:

→ Stop treating AI adoption as optional professional development. Make it part of how work gets done.  
→ Find the people on your team who are genuinely excited about this stuff. Give them room to experiment and teach others.  
→ Accept that the workflow you perfected three weeks ago might already be outdated. That's not failure—that's the pace we're operating at now.  
→ Create space for controlled experimentation without blowing up client delivery.  

The tension between innovation and stability isn't going away. There's no clean resolution.

But the agencies that figure out how to keep teams adopting new approaches—while still hitting deadlines and delivering results—are going to have a massive advantage.

The constraint isn't access to technology anymore.

It's whether your team will actually use it.

---

## Key Insight
The success of AI in business depends more on team adoption and workflow integration than on the tools themselves.

## Supporting Observation
Organizations that actively change how they work outperform those that only adopt tools without changing behavior.
